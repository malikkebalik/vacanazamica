Career

She established herself in New York, where she took piano lessons from Chilean pianist Claudio Arrau in 1944.

She made her first New York acting appearance in the English-language premiere of Federico Garcia Lorca’s If Five Years Pass at the Provincetown Playhouse in April 1945.

She made her Broadway debut on July 20, 1946, at The Booth Theatre as the ingénue in Ben Hecht’s Swan Song.

Montealegre appeared with symphony orchestras throughout the United States in dramatic acting and narrating roles, including collaborations with her husband, American composer and conductor Leonard Bernstein.
