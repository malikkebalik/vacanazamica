Scott Nogrady Obituary: Jammer at Scooter Slowgrady dies at the age of 53

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Tuesday, August 6, 2024

Ross Township, Pennsylvania – Scott Charles Nogrady, aged 53, of Ross Township, Pennsylvania, passed away peacefully on October 7, 2023, surrounded by family and friends.

He was the devoted and beloved husband for 25 years to Lisa Nogrady and a loving father to Michael Scott Nogrady. Scott was the son of Elmer (Barbara Looney) Nogrady and the late Audrey Nogrady, and a brother to Audra (Thomas) Miller, Kimberly (Gary) Stifter, and Colleen (William Start) Drache. He was also a beloved son-in-law to Rita Schwarzmeier and the late James D. Schwarzmeier, and a brother-in-law to Lori (Randy) Geche and Jim (Julie) Schwarzmeier. Scott was a cherished uncle to Douglas Morgan, Gregory Morgan, Justin Miller, Andy Geche, and Luella Schwarzmeier. He was also a loving cat-dad to Toby and Olaf.

Scott was born on April 1, 1970, in Passaic, New Jersey, and developed a love for the outdoors, enjoying skiing, bicycling, camping, and dirt-biking in Vernon. After relocating to Pittsburgh in the late 1980s, he began his career as a Registered Nurse at UPMC Shadyside Hospital and UPMC Homecare. Later, he completed an apprenticeship to become a journeyman lineman, taking great pride in his work at Sargent Electric and Duquesne Light Company.

Being a dedicated father to Michael, he volunteered for various activities, ranging from assisting with Michael’s Boy Scout troop to being the “buzzer guy” and scorekeeper for Holy Cross Academy basketball and volleyball games. He was also a volunteer firefighter for West View Borough. Scott was known for his helpful nature and willingness to support family and friends in every way possible.

Scott had an unparalleled love for music, playing guitar and singing every day. He frequently performed with dear friends, relishing the joy of playing or simply listening with a drink in hand. His love for adventure led him to indulge in dirt biking and mountain biking, traveling across the country and attending rallies where he often provided musical entertainment, forming lasting friendships. Scott’s favorite retreat was his camp in the mountains of West Virginia, where he created cherished memories with loved ones.

Scott embraced life to the fullest, illuminating every room with his bright smile and welcoming demeanor. He was a devoted family man, expressing immense love for his wife and son, always grateful for the beautiful life they had built together. Scott was deeply loved by many and will be profoundly missed.

Friends and family will gather at Schellhaas Funeral Home, 388 Center Avenue, West View (Pittsburgh), Pennsylvania 15229. Receiving hours are Thursday, October 12, 2023, from 12-4pm and 6-9pm, and again on Friday, October 13, 2023, from 10-11am, followed by a service.

In lieu of flowers, please consider a donation to The Humane Animal Rescue of Pittsburgh.
