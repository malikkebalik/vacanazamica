Who is Savannah Guthrie?

Savannah Guthrie is a journalist as well as an attorney her birth name is Savannah Clark Guthrie-Feldman. She worked as the main co-anchor of the NBC News morning show, Today.

Similarly, she also joined NBC News in September 2007.

Savannah Guthrie: Early Professional Career

Talking about her professional, Savannah Guthrie began her career with ABC affiliate KMIZ, in Columbia, Missouri. Likewise, she also worked there for two years and returned to Tucson and a job with NBC affiliate KVOA in 1995.

Then, after receiving five years of experience in Arizona, she joined WRC-TV, Washington, D.C. where she also covered major stories including the September 11, 2001, attack on The Pentagon and the 2001 anthrax attacks.

Whereas she also became a correspondent for NBC News in September 2007, as an NBC correspondent, she covered Sarah Palin‘s 2008 vice-presidential race from Fairbanks, Pittsburgh, San Antonio, Sioux City, Iowa, and Washington. Moreover, she was also named White House correspondent for NBC News on December 18, 2007. Similarly, she contributed to all NBC News properties in this capacity.

Therefore, she conducted an interview with Donald Trump in 2011, in which he discussed his role in the Barack Obama “birther” controversy. Likewise, she also reported exclusive details about the Death of Osama bin Laden. Whereas, she also played alongside compatriot Jack Sock in an exhibition tennis match in 2018.
