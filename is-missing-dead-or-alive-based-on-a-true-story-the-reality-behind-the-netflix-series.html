Is Missing: Dead or Alive Based on a True Story? The Reality Behind the Netflix Series

Posted by Martina Birk on Friday, September 20, 2024

Introduction

Missing: Dead or Alive is a new Netflix series that follows a team of police investigators in South Carolina as they work on four different missing person cases. The series shows the challenges and complexities of finding people who have vanished without a trace, and the emotional impact on their families and friends. But is Missing: Dead or Alive based on a true story? How much of the series is factual, and how much is dramatized? Here is what we found out.

The Cases

All of the cases depicted in Missing: Dead or Alive are real cases that occurred in South Carolina between 2019 and 2021. Each of the cases was investigated by the Missing Persons Unit in the Richland County Sheriff’s Department. The cases are:

  • Lorraine Garcia, a 61-year-old woman who disappeared after her son tried to sell her house without her consent. She was later found alive, but refused to reveal her whereabouts or contact her family.
  • Amirah Watson, a 10-year-old girl who was abducted by her mother, who had lost custody of her. She was later found in Georgia by the U.S. Marshals, and reunited with her father.
  • David Taylor, a 38-year-old man who went missing after winning $10,000 in the lottery. He was later found dead in a motel room, with signs of drug use and foul play.
  • Sierra Stevens, a 19-year-old woman who disappeared after leaving a party with a stranger. She was later found alive, but traumatized and addicted to drugs.

The Sources

The series claims to be based on real events, and uses footage from the actual investigations, such as interviews, interrogations, searches, and surveillance. However, some scenes and dialogues may have been recreated or edited for dramatic purposes. The series also uses narration from one of the investigators, Vicki Rains, who provides commentary and insight on the cases. However, some viewers have questioned the authenticity and accuracy of the narration, and whether it reflects the actual thoughts and feelings of the investigator.

The series also relies on some secondary sources, such as news articles, reports, and websites, to provide background information and context on the cases. However, some of these sources may not be reliable or up-to-date, and may contain errors or inconsistencies. For example, some websites have used the incorrect spelling of the names of the missing persons, such as “Michael Jorden” or “Michael Jordan” instead of “Mike Haggar”.

Conclusion

Missing: Dead or Alive is based on a true story, but it is not a completely accurate or objective representation of the reality. The series uses real footage and cases, but it also uses some creative techniques and elements to enhance the drama and suspense. The series also uses some sources that may not be trustworthy or verified, and may have some mistakes or biases. Therefore, viewers should not take everything they see or hear in the series as the truth, and should do their own research and verification if they want to learn more about the cases. Is Missing: Dead or Alive based on a true story? The answer is yes, but not entirely.
