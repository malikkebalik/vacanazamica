Overall Meaning

In Kali Uchis's song "Moonlight," the author expresses a desire to escape from the mundane routine of everyday life by getting high with their lover. The lyrics are reflective of the sense of longing and desire to be free from the weight of the world, the pressure and the stress, and just be with each other. The first verse is about the desire to get high and live carefree with the singer's lover, as they stare at themselves in the mirror and feel like they’re looking at a doll. The chorus repeats the desire to get high with their lover, kissing them, feeling like a doll, and going out in the moonlight.


The second verse is about how the singer's lover takes care of them and is always there for them, despite the stress they put on their lover's brain. The pre-chorus emphasizes the importance of peace of mind and being able to let go of everything, which the singer feels when they are with their lover, making them their priority. The chorus repeats the sentiment that they just want to get high with their lover, kissing them, feeling like a doll, and going out in the moonlight.


Overall, the lyrics of "Moonlight" express a desire for escapism from the pressures and stresses of everyday life, to find peace and freedom in the company of a lover.
